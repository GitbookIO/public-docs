# Agent quickstart

> **Tutorial.** Follow every step in order — at the end your agent will have opened its first change request in GitBook.

## Before you begin

You need a GitBook account with edit access to a space, and an MCP-capable client (Claude, Cursor, or similar). No code is assumed.

## Step 1: Add the GitBook MCP server to your client

Point your client at GitBook's MCP endpoint. Each client has a one-line setup — pick yours and paste it.

## Step 2: Authenticate

Complete the browser sign-in when your client prompts you. Your agent now acts with your GitBook permissions.

## Step 3: Ask your agent to explore your docs

Prompt your agent to list your spaces and read a page. This verifies the connection end to end before you change anything.

## Step 4: Make your first change

Ask your agent to fix a typo or improve one paragraph, as a change request. The agent drafts the edit and opens the change request for you.

## You did it 🎉

Open GitBook and find the change request your agent created — review and merge it. That's the loop: prompt, review, merge. Next: [No MCP? Use skills directly](no-mcp-use-skills-directly.md) or explore [common workflows](../gitbook-mcp/common-workflows.md).
