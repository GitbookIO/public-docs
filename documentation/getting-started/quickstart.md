# Quickstart

> **Tutorial.** Follow every step in order — at the end you'll have a live site you can share.

## Before you begin

You need a GitBook account (free plan is fine) and about ten minutes. Nothing else is assumed.

## Step 1: Create your docs site

From the GitBook dashboard, create a new docs site. GitBook creates the site and its first section with an empty space for your content.

## Step 2: Add your first pages

Open the editor and create a homepage plus two or three pages. Use headings and a hint block so you can see how blocks work.

## Step 3: Organize your navigation

Drag pages in the sidebar to set their order, and group related pages. This structure becomes your published site's navigation.

## Step 4: Preview your site

Use the preview to see exactly what visitors will see, on desktop and mobile widths.

## Step 5: Publish

Open your site's settings, set the audience (public), and hit Publish.

## You're live 🎉

Your site is now published at `yourname.gitbook.io/your-site`. Open the URL in a private browser window — that's what the world sees. Next: [set a custom domain](../learn/publishing/custom-domain/) or [invite your team](../learn/collaboration/invite-and-manage-members.md).
